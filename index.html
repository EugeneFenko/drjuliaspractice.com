    Welcome to Dr. Julia's practice!

    The decision to seek psychotherapy is not always easy, so I want to applaud you for taking this first important step! In our work together, my goal is to increase your awareness of how learned thoughts, emotions and behaviors can create psychological pain and even physical symptoms. We will develop your ability to manage these obstacles in order to live a more happy and fulfilling life. Stress, emotional pain, and physical discomfort do not have to get in your way of having healthy relationships, giving your best performance at work/school, or achieving your goals!

    Today we know that the mind and body are interconnected. I draw from my training in health psychology and behavioral medicine, cognitive-behavioral therapy, mindfulness, and other therapeutic modalities to treat a wide variety of psychological and behavioral issues using a holistic mind-body approach.

    Although I use research-supported methods, I believe that each individual is unique, as are his/her past experiences, current concerns, and environment. We will work together to develop a treatment plan that is specifically designed to meet your needs and goals.

    Whether you're struggling to get out of bed in the morning, or can benefit from simply speaking with a non-biased professional, in a supporting and caring setting, don't wait. Take control of your life and call today.

    About Dr. Julia

    I have been captivated by the field of psychology since my first day in an “Introduction to Psychology” course in college. I graduated from UC Berkeley with a Bachelor’s degree in Psychology and high honors Summa Cum Laude in 2005. I subsequently received my Master’s and Doctorate degrees in Clinical-Community Psychology from an APA Accredited program at the University of La Verne.

    I have trained and worked in various clinical settings, including UCLA’s Resnick Neuropsychiatric Hospital & Semel Institute, Verdugo Mental Health Center, and The Help Group. After completing an APA psychology internship at the Loma Linda VA Medical Center, I went on to complete a year-long postdoctoral fellowship at the Miami VA Medical Center. Before returning to sunny California and transitioning into private practice, I was employed as a clinical psychologist at the Orlando VA Medical Center.

    My time spent at the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) working with current and former service members of the military has allowed me to master the most current treatment techniques for a wide spectrum of psychological and behavioral conditions. I can provide a safe, confidential environment where we will work collaboratively to address your current concerns.

    In my free time, I enjoy traveling, learning about different cultures, doing yoga, and spending time outdoors.

    Services

    I use several types of psychotherapies and techniques such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT), Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT), and Motivational Interviewing (MI) to treat the following:

    • Mood disorders: reduction of symptoms of depression, anxiety, bipolar
    • Trauma-related disorders (Specialized training/VA regional certification in treatment of PTSD)
    • Stress management and relaxation training
    • Health and illness concerns: Coping with acute and chronic health conditions
    • Relationship concerns
    • Life transitions including aging, grief, and loss
    • Multicultural issues, gender issues
    • Substance use/Smoking cessation treatment
    • Sleep-related problems
    • Chronic pain management

    Additional treatment/services provided:

    • Family/caregiver support – Management of stress related to caring for a family member
    • Cognitive training – Learning of compensatory cognitive strategies to assist in coping with decline in memory, attention, visuospatial ability, processing speed, and executive function related to aging and/or illness.

    Individual Therapy

    Individual therapy, also known as “psychotherapy” or “counseling," entails one-on-one sessions with Dr. Julia, typically occurring on a weekly basis and lasting 50 minutes. During this time, clients are encouraged to explore their thoughts, feelings, and patterns of behavior in a safe, confidential, and caring setting. Clients seek therapy for a variety of reasons, ranging from traumatic childhood experiences, to managing anxiety and depression, to seeking greater personal growth and development.



    Group Therapy

    Group therapy typically involves weekly 90-minute sessions. During this time, Dr. Julia meets face-to-face with a small group of clients (generally five to ten members in a group). Group members are selected based on their desire to work on similar concerns and/or issues, such as stress management, depression or anxiety, substance abuse, or anger management. Group therapy provides a unique opportunity to share and learn from others, who are struggling with similar concerns. Group members have often voiced having benefited from mutual support, understanding, and gathering of valuable resources. Dr. Julia is currently offering the following weekly groups: Stress/Anxiety Management and Depression Management. Groups are offered in the Beverly Hills and Sherman Oaks areas.

    Couples Therapy

    Couples therapy is a process through which a couple works with Dr. Julia to identify areas of conflict, as well as strengths in their relationship. Therapeutic goals are then established to improve important aspects that are known to lead to greater satisfaction and happiness in the relationship. Commonly addressed issues include poor communication, rebuilding of trust, emotional unavailability, and maladaptive behaviors.
